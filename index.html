Help Kit

Free, clear, source-cited guides for moments that matter — so anyone can recognize an emergency and act in time.

Many acute emergencies can be spotted earlier or made less harmful. These short guides distill guidance from public health authorities and trusted medical/public-safety sources into plain language anyone can use, print, and share. No sign-up, no tracking, public domain.

Offline tip

Before a storm, heat wave, wildfire, or other outage, open this site once on each phone while online. In modern browsers, the pages and PDFs are saved for offline use after that first visit. Also download or print the combined PDF pack for a backup.

How to use these

  • Localize before distributing — use the localization checklist and share copy to verify emergency numbers, access details, and official local resources.
  • Read on any phone — pages are mobile-friendly and load quickly.
  • Save for offline use — open the site once while online so a modern browser can cache the pages and PDFs; test before relying on it during an outage.
  • Print a handout — each guide has a clean print layout and a downloadable PDF; the combined print pack includes all current one-pagers for paper backup.
  • Share freely — everything here is public domain (CC0). Copy it, translate it, reprint it, no permission needed.

For community organizers & mutual-aid groups

These pages are built to be reused. The full source is on GitHub — fork it, adapt the wording for your region's emergency numbers and local resources, and host your own copy.

These guides are general information, not medical advice, and are not a substitute for professional emergency care or training. In an emergency, always call your local emergency number, and verify against your local health authority. Created as an open public-good project.
