Help Kit

Free, clear, source-cited guides for moments that matter — so anyone can recognize a preventable emergency and act in time.

Many acute emergencies can be prevented, spotted earlier, or made less harmful. These short guides distill guidance from public health authorities and trusted medical/public-safety sources into plain language anyone can use, print, and share. No sign-up, no tracking, public domain.

How to use these

  • Localize before distributing — use the localization checklist and share copy to verify emergency numbers, access details, and official local resources.
  • Read on any phone — pages are mobile-friendly and load instantly.
  • Print a handout — each guide has a clean print layout and a downloadable PDF, ideal for posting in a break room, shelter, library, or community space.
  • Share freely — everything here is public domain (CC0). Copy it, translate it, reprint it, no permission needed.

For community organizers & mutual-aid groups

These pages are built to be reused. The full source is on GitHub — fork it, adapt the wording for your region's emergency numbers and local resources, and host your own copy. More topics may be added over time.

These guides are general information, not medical advice, and are not a substitute for professional emergency care or training. In an emergency, always call your local emergency number, and verify against your local health authority. Created as an open public-good project.
