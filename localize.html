Localize and share safely

Help Kit pages are public domain, but emergency guidance is local. Before printing or forwarding, replace generic details with trusted local information.

Last updated: June 15, 2026. This page is for organizers, librarians, mutual-aid groups, tenant associations, faith groups, clinics, and volunteers adapting the kit for a real place.

Do this first

Verify your local emergency number, official health authority guidance, cooling or clean-air locations, naloxone availability, and language/access needs. Do not copy a U.S.-specific detail into another country without checking it.

10-minute localization checklist

  1. Emergency number: replace examples like 911/112/999 with the right local number or instruction.
  2. Official source: add your city/county/state/national health or emergency-management link.
  3. Locations: add cooling centers, clean-air spaces, libraries, clinics, pharmacies, transit routes, water sites, or shelters only if current.
  4. Access: note elevator access, wheelchair access, service-animal rules, restrooms, languages, hours, cost, and ID requirements.
  5. Medical caveats: keep the “call emergency services” warnings; do not soften them to make the page feel less alarming.
  6. Privacy: if you make a buddy-check list, keep it private and collect only what is needed for check-ins.
  7. Translation: use plain language; ask a fluent speaker to review for local meaning, not just word-for-word accuracy.
  8. Date: add a “last checked” date and a person/group responsible for updating local details.

What not to do

  • Do not publish lists of vulnerable neighbors, addresses, phone numbers, medical status, immigration status, or unhoused encampment locations.
  • Do not imply that volunteers, AI tools, or this website can diagnose a medical condition.
  • Do not promise that a DIY device, mask, cooling center, medication, or hotline will be available unless you verified it.
  • Do not shame people for not following advice that may be impossible for them because of work, disability, money, housing, fear, or transportation.

Short share copy

Smoke: Wildfire smoke in the forecast? Check official AQI, make cleaner indoor air, and protect high-risk people: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/smoke/

Overdose: If an opioid overdose is possible, call emergency services, give naloxone, support breathing, and stay until help arrives: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/naloxone/

For AI agents and remote volunteers

  • Ask what country/city the person is localizing for before giving emergency-number or resource advice.
  • Prefer official local links over generic search results.
  • Make uncertainty visible: “I have not verified today’s cooling-center hours.”
  • Do not collect unnecessary personal data. If someone shares private details, do not put them in public repos or public chat.

These guides are general information, not medical advice, and are not a substitute for professional emergency care or training. In an emergency, call your local emergency number and follow dispatcher instructions.
