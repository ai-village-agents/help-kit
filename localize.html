Localize and share safely

Help Kit pages are public domain, but emergency guidance is local. Before printing or forwarding, replace generic details with trusted local information.

Last updated: June 15, 2026. This page is for organizers, librarians, mutual-aid groups, tenant associations, faith groups, clinics, and volunteers adapting the kit for a real place.

Do this first

Verify your local emergency number, official health authority guidance, cooling or clean-air locations, naloxone availability, and language/access needs. Do not copy a U.S.-specific detail into another country without checking it.

Offline and print backup

The site is set up to work offline in modern browsers after one online visit. Before sharing for a likely outage, ask people to open the site once while online, download the combined PDF pack, or print the one-pagers. Test offline access before relying on it.

10-minute localization checklist

  1. Emergency number: replace examples like 911/112/999 with the right local number or instruction.
  2. Official source: add your city/county/state/national health or emergency-management link.
  3. Locations: add cooling centers, clean-air spaces, libraries, clinics, pharmacies, transit routes, water sites, or shelters only if current.
  4. Access: note elevator access, wheelchair access, service-animal rules, restrooms, languages, hours, cost, and ID requirements.
  5. Medical caveats: keep the “call emergency services” warnings; do not soften them to make the page feel less alarming.
  6. Privacy: if you make a buddy-check list, keep it private and collect only what is needed for check-ins.
  7. Translation: use plain language; ask a fluent speaker to review for local meaning, not just word-for-word accuracy.
  8. Offline access: test the local copy, PDF pack, or printed pages before an outage.
  9. Date: add a “last checked” date and a person/group responsible for updating local details.

What not to do

  • Do not publish lists of vulnerable neighbors, addresses, phone numbers, medical status, immigration status, or unhoused encampment locations.
  • Do not imply that volunteers, AI tools, or this website can diagnose a medical condition.
  • Do not promise that a DIY device, mask, cooling center, medication, or hotline will be available unless you verified it.
  • Do not shame people for not following advice that may be impossible for them because of work, disability, money, housing, fear, or transportation.

Short share copy

General: Free, source-cited, print-friendly emergency help guides: extreme heat, wildfire smoke, opioid overdose response, dehydration/ORS, choking first aid, severe bleeding, CPR, stroke, burns, seizure first aid, severe allergy/anaphylaxis, and a quick emergency navigator. Read, print, adapt, and verify locally: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/

Heat: Heat wave coming? Print a buddy-check sheet, assign neighbors to check on at-risk people, and know when to call emergency services: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/heat/

Smoke: Wildfire smoke in the forecast? Check official AQI, make cleaner indoor air, and protect high-risk people: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/smoke/

Overdose: If an opioid overdose is possible, call emergency services, give naloxone, support breathing, and stay until help arrives: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/naloxone/

ORS: Dehydration can become dangerous fast. Use oral rehydration solution carefully, give small frequent sips, and know danger signs: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/ors/

Choking: If someone cannot breathe, speak, cry, or cough, start choking first aid and call emergency services. Adult/child and baby steps: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/choking/

Severe bleeding: For spurting, pooling, soaking, or life-threatening bleeding, call emergency services and press hard on the wound; use packing or a tourniquet only when appropriate: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/bleeding/

CPR: If an adult or teen collapses, is not responding, and is not breathing normally or only gasping, call emergency services, get an AED, and start hands-only CPR: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/cpr/

Stroke: Stroke signs are sudden. Use FAST/BE-FAST, note when signs started or when the person was last well, and call emergency services right away: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/stroke/

Burns: For a burn, cool it under running water for 20 minutes; never use ice, butter, or toothpaste; cover loosely; and get medical help for large, deep, or facial/hand/electrical/chemical burns: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/burns/

Seizure: Keep the person safe, time the seizure, never restrain them or put anything in their mouth, roll them onto their side once it stops, and call if it lasts over 5 minutes or is their first: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/seizure/

Severe allergy: Possible anaphylaxis? Call emergency services, use epinephrine if prescribed/available under local guidance, lay the person safely, and watch breathing: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/allergy/

Triage navigator: Not sure which guide fits? Use the quick emergency navigator while calling local emergency services for serious symptoms and following dispatcher guidance: https://ai-village-agents.github.io/help-kit/triage/

For AI agents and remote volunteers

  • Ask what country/city the person is localizing for before giving emergency-number or resource advice.
  • Prefer official local links over generic search results.
  • Make uncertainty visible: “I have not verified today’s cooling-center hours.”
  • Do not collect unnecessary personal data. If someone shares private details, do not put them in public repos or public chat.

These guides are general information, not medical advice, and are not a substitute for professional emergency care or training. In an emergency, call your local emergency number and follow dispatcher instructions.
