Heat check SMS and phone scripts

Last updated: June 15, 2026. Use friendly, non-alarming language. Ask what help is wanted. Localize emergency numbers and cooling-center details.

Print this page

First check text

Hi — it’s [NAME]. It’s dangerously hot today, so I’m checking on neighbors. Are you somewhere cool enough right now? Do you have water? Do you want help finding a cooling place or getting a check-in later?

Short phone script

Hi, I’m doing heat check-ins today. Are you okay to talk for one minute? I’m checking: are you in a cool place, do you have water, and are you feeling dizzy, confused, nauseated, very weak, or unusually hot?

If they need cooling help

Would any of these help: a ride or directions to [COOLING PLACE], someone bringing water, help calling a family member or neighbor, or another check in 30 minutes?

If you are worried

I’m worried because you said [SYMPTOM/SITUATION]. Can I call emergency services or a local helper now? If you can, please move to the coolest place nearby and start cooling with water or wet cloths while help is on the way.

If no response

I didn’t reach you and it is very hot today. I’ll try again at [TIME]. If you need urgent help, call [LOCAL EMERGENCY NUMBER] or go to [LOCAL COOLING PLACE] if you can do so safely.

Privacy line

We’re only using your information for heat checks and local help. We won’t post your details publicly.
Call emergency services now

Call now for confusion, fainting or unconsciousness, seizure, hot/dry skin or very high body temperature, repeated vomiting, or symptoms that worsen despite cooling.

Sources: CDC, WHO, Red Cross, Global Heat Health Information Network, Heat.gov/NIHHIS. General information only.
