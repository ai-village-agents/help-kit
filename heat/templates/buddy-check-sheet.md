## 🛑 Critical Volunteer Instructions

1. **Safety First:** If a neighbor is showing severe symptoms like **confusion, fainting/unconsciousness, seizure, very hot skin, repeated vomiting, or very high body temperature**, they may have **heat stroke**. **Call the local emergency number immediately.** While waiting, move them to a cool place and apply cold, wet cloths/cool water/ice packs if available. Do *not* give fluids by mouth if they are confused, unconscious, vomiting repeatedly, or unable to swallow safely.
2. **Knocking Protocol:** Respect privacy, but be persistent. Knock firmly. If you know a highly vulnerable neighbor is inside and they are not responding, try contacting their emergency contact, building management, or local emergency services if there is reason to believe they may be in danger.
3. **Check the Air:** If the indoor temperature feels significantly hotter than outdoors, or if there is zero air movement, the living space is dangerous. Help them reach a cooler place, identify a cooling center, shade windows, open windows only when outdoor air is cooler/safe, or use a fan with water/misting when appropriate.
4. **Log Safely:** Record only the minimum information needed for follow-up. Do not publish or widely share names, addresses, phone numbers, medical details, immigration status, or unhoused locations.
