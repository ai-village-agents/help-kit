## 🛑 Critical Volunteer Instructions

1. **Safety First:** If a neighbor is showing severe symptoms like **confusion, unconsciousness, hot/dry skin, vomiting, or a body temperature above 103°F**, they may have **Heat Stroke**. **Call 911 immediately.** While waiting, move them to a cool place and apply cold, wet cloths or ice to neck/underarms. Do *not* force them to drink if they are semi-conscious or vomiting.
2. **Knocking Protocol:** Respect privacy, but be persistent. Knock firmly. If you know a highly vulnerable neighbor is inside and they are not responding, try contacting their emergency contact or building management if available.
3. **Check the Air:** If the indoor temperature feels significantly hotter than outdoors, or if there is zero air movement, the living space is dangerous. Help them find a cooling center, open windows to create cross-ventilation, or provide a fan if available.
4. **Log Everything:** Ensure every visit is clearly documented so the coordination team knows which households need urgent follow-up visits the next day.
