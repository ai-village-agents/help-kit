# KEEP COOL, STAY SAFE! ☀️🌡️
### Extreme Heat Emergency Safety Guide — Save This Page

Extreme heat can be highly dangerous. When temperatures rise, simple actions can reduce risk and get help sooner. Please read, share, and post this flyer in visible community areas.

---

## 🕒 3 GOLDEN RULES OF HEAT SAFETY

### 1. HYDRATE, HYDRATE, HYDRATE 💧
* Drink water **even if you do not feel thirsty**. Drink regularly and take extra water in heavy heat or sweating; if a doctor limits your fluids, ask what is safe for you.
* Avoid alcohol; do not rely mainly on very sugary drinks. Caffeine affects people differently—choose water or electrolytes if you feel unwell or are sweating heavily.
* If you are sweating heavily for a long time, replace salts too: use ORS/electrolytes, a sports drink, or food with salt plus water if appropriate.

### 2. KEEP YOUR ENVIRONMENT COOL 🏠
* **Create Cross-Ventilation:** Keep windows and blinds closed when outdoor air is hotter than indoor air; open them when outdoor air becomes cooler and safe.
* **The Wet Sheet Trick:** Damp skin or cloths and use a fan for evaporative cooling if safe; keep electrical devices away from water and cords out of walkways.
* **Foot Baths & Sprays:** Soak your feet in cool water or use a spray bottle to damp your skin. Evaporating water can help cool the body.
* **Limit Appliance Use:** Avoid using ovens, stoves, or dryers, as they emit immense heat into your home.

### 3. CHECK IN ON OTHERS 🤝
* **Buddy Up:** Call or visit older adults, people living alone, unhoused neighbors, outdoor workers, and people with health conditions at least daily during severe heat if safe and welcomed.
* **Never Leave Anyone in Cars:** Never leave children, pets, or vulnerable individuals in a parked car, even with windows cracked. Temperatures inside cars can spike to lethal levels in minutes.

## ⚕️ HOW TO SPOT HEAT ILLNESS: KNOW THE WARNING SIGNS

### HEAT EXHAUSTION 🌡️ (Act Quick!)
* **Skin:** Cool, pale, and clammy. Heavy sweating.
* **Symptoms:** Muscle cramps, dizziness, headache, nausea, weakness, fast pulse.
* **Action:** Move to a cool place. Loosen clothing. Sip water slowly. Put cool, wet cloths on your skin or take a cool bath. **If symptoms worsen or do not improve in 1 hour, seek medical help.**

### HEAT STROKE 🚨 (LIFE-THREATENING EMERGENCY!)
* **Skin:** Hot, red, dry OR damp. Very high body temperature (if you can measure it, 103°F / 39.4°C or higher).
* **Symptoms:** **Confusion**, dizziness, slurred speech, rapid strong pulse, vomiting, loss of consciousness.
* **Action:** **CALL YOUR LOCAL EMERGENCY NUMBER IMMEDIATELY.** Move the person to a cool area. Do NOT give anything to drink if they are confused, unconscious, vomiting repeatedly, or unable to swallow safely. Cool them down immediately with wet cloths, ice packs, or a cool bath until help arrives.
