# KEEP COOL, STAY SAFE! ☀️🌡️
### Extreme Heat Emergency Safety Guide — Save This Page

Extreme heat can be highly dangerous. When temperatures rise, simple actions can save lives. Please read, share, and post this flyer in visible community areas.

---

## 🕒 3 GOLDEN RULES OF HEAT SAFETY

### 1. HYDRATE, HYDRATE, HYDRATE 💧
* Drink water **even if you do not feel thirsty**. Aim for at least 2 to 4 cups of water every hour during peak heat.
* Avoid sugary, caffeinated, or alcoholic beverages, as they can accelerate dehydration.
* If you are sweating heavily, supplement with sports drinks or electrolytes to replenish minerals.

### 2. KEEP YOUR ENVIRONMENT COOL 🏠
* **Create Cross-Ventilation:** Keep windows closed during the hottest hours (11:00 AM – 5:00 PM) to lock cool air in. Open them in the evening when the temperature drops.
* **The Wet Sheet Trick:** Hang wet sheets in front of open windows or fans to cool incoming air via evaporation.
* **Foot Baths & Sprays:** Soak your feet in cool water or use a spray bottle to damp your skin. Evaporating water is an extremely efficient cooling mechanism.
* **Limit Appliance Use:** Avoid using ovens, stoves, or dryers, as they emit immense heat into your home.

### 3. CHECK IN ON OTHERS 🤝
* **Buddy Up:** Call or visit elderly neighbors, unhoused community members, and those with underlying health conditions twice a day.
* **Never Leave Anyone in Cars:** Never leave children, pets, or vulnerable individuals in a parked car, even with windows cracked. Temperatures inside cars can spike to lethal levels in minutes.

## ⚕️ HOW TO SPOT HEAT ILLNESS: KNOW THE WARNING SIGNS

### HEAT EXHAUSTION 🌡️ (Act Quick!)
* **Skin:** Cool, pale, and clammy. Heavy sweating.
* **Symptoms:** Muscle cramps, dizziness, headache, nausea, weakness, fast pulse.
* **Action:** Move to a cool place. Loosen clothing. Sip water slowly. Put cool, wet cloths on your skin or take a cool bath. **If symptoms worsen or do not improve in 1 hour, seek medical help.**

### HEAT STROKE 🚨 (LIFE-THREATENING EMERGENCY!)
* **Skin:** Hot, red, dry OR damp. **Body temperature over 103°F (39.4°C)**.
* **Symptoms:** **Confusion**, dizziness, slurred speech, rapid strong pulse, vomiting, loss of consciousness.
* **Action:** **CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY.** Move the person to a cool area. Do NOT give them anything to drink. Cool them down immediately with wet cloths, ice packs, or a cool bath until help arrives.
