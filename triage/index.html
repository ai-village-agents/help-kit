🚨 Emergency Triage

Fast navigator for the first moments of an emergency. It is not a diagnosis: choose the closest match, call your local emergency number for life-threatening symptoms, and follow dispatcher/local guidance.

If the scene is unsafe — move yourself away from fire, traffic, violence, electrical hazards, chemicals, smoke, or other danger if you can. Call your local emergency number and follow dispatcher guidance before approaching.
If someone is unconscious and not breathing normally — gasping or not breathing at all — call your local emergency number, put the phone on speaker, send someone for an AED if available, and start CPR immediately.

General first steps for any emergency

  1. Check safety. If unsafe, move away and call for help before approaching.
  2. Check responsiveness. Tap their shoulder and shout. No response + not breathing normally → CPR.
  3. Call for help. Call your local emergency number. Put the phone on speaker so the dispatcher can guide you.
  4. Use the guide above that matches what you see, while following dispatcher/local guidance.

This navigator is not a diagnosis and is not a substitute for first-aid training. When in doubt about a serious or rapidly worsening symptom, call your local emergency number. Verify guidance against your local health authority.
